Separation Creek Field Stay

A unique off-grid field stay within a lovingly restored 1940s bondwood caravan, set across seven acres of protected bushland at Separation Creek on Victoria’s Great Ocean Road. Coastal views, forest bathing, wildlife, and quiet immersion in a rare bushland corridor between township and National Park.

A small place within a larger landscape

The sanctuary is located approximately 150km west of Melbourne at Separation Creek, just beyond the coastal town of Lorne along the Great Ocean Road. The land forms a narrow corridor between the township and the National Park, supporting dense bushland, native grasses, koalas, kangaroos, wallabies, and a wide range of birdlife. The caravan has been carefully restored to retain the character of the original 1940s bondwood structure while providing simple, quiet comfort within a protected landscape.

Forest bathing • Wildlife • Coastal air

The stay sits within a private nature reserve where the surrounding forest is being gradually restored through indigenous planting, habitat creation, and careful land management. Guests are invited to experience the landscape slowly — walking, sitting, observing, and resting within the bushland.

There is no television, no street lighting, and no urban noise. Only wind through the trees, ocean air moving up the valley, and the changing light across the Otway coast.
