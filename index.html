Separation Creek • Field Stay

Off-grid accommodation within coastal forest.

A unique off-grid field stay within a lovingly restored 1940s bondwood caravan, set across seven acres of protected bushland at Separation Creek on Victoria’s Great Ocean Road.

A small stay within a larger landscape.

Seven acres of protected bushland forming a corridor between the National Park and the township of Separation Creek. Wildlife moves freely through the site, and the atmosphere is quiet, coastal, and deeply restorative.

A restored 1940s bondwood caravan.

The caravan has been carefully restored to retain its original character while offering warmth, comfort, and a quiet place to stay within the bushland.

Forest bathing, wildlife, and coastal calm.

A place for walking, sitting, reading, and slowing down. Birdlife, wind, ocean air, and changing light shape the experience of the stay.

Stay details

Seven acres of bushland · wildlife corridor · off-grid retreat · coastal landscape

Arrival

11 Dollar Track, Separation Creek. Follow the driveway past the water tank and walk toward the ocean to the caravan.

Booking

Separation Creek is part of the Gardener & Son field sites program.

This field stay is generally reserved for clients, collaborators, and project partners. Limited stays may be offered at certain times through invitation or special booking periods.

Separation Creek is the first of several Gardener & Son field sites, with future plans for field school gatherings, forest bathing sessions, ecological workshops, and small off-grid cabins held carefully within the landscape.
