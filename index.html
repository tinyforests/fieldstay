Separation Creek Stay

At the nature reserve, in a lovingly restored 1940s bondwood caravan, enjoy forest bathing, coastal air, wildlife, and quiet off-grid living.

A small place within a larger landscape

Hidden within regenerating coastal forest at Separation Creek, this small off-grid stay offers a quiet experience of nature. The caravan has been carefully restored using original materials, allowing the character of the 1940s structure to remain intact, while providing simple comfort within a protected landscape.

Forest bathing • Wildlife • Coastal views

The stay sits within a private nature reserve where the surrounding forest is being gradually restored using indigenous planting, habitat creation, and careful land management. Kangaroos, birds, insects, and native vegetation move freely through the site, and guests are invited to experience the landscape slowly — walking, sitting, observing, and resting.

There is no television, no street lighting, and no urban noise. Only wind through the trees, ocean air, and the changing light across the Otway coast.

Off-grid accommodation by Gardener & Son

This stay forms part of a longer-term project exploring ecological restoration, private nature reserves, and small-scale regenerative accommodation.
